Quick Overview of GANs (+SRGANs)

An SRGAN is a type of GAN (Generative Adversarial Network) that has 2 neural networks (NN): a generator and discriminator. To keep it short, these two NN compete against each other where the generator tries to create ‘real’ data and the discriminator needs to recognize whether the data is real or fake.

The Super Resolution-GAN aims at taking a low-resolution (LR) image and being able to upscale it to a higher-resolution (a super- resolution — SR). The objective that we want a GAN to achieve is to minimize the error made by the generator so that we can generate as much ‘real’ data as possible.

During training, we provide the Generator with two inputs: an LR and HR input. Only during training is the HR input available to the Generator because the goal is to take the LR input and learn how to make its output as identical to the HR input.
